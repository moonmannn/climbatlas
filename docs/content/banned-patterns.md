# ClimbAtlas Patterns to Review

These are review triggers, not a blind word blacklist. Keep a phrase only when it adds concrete meaning and no plainer version works as well.

## Generic marketing frames

Flag:

- `where X meets Y`
- `more than just`
- `isn't just`
- `whether you're X or Y`
- `waiting to be discovered`
- `hidden gem`
- `breathtaking`
- `unforgettable adventure`
- `every route tells a story`
- `playground for climbers`
- `something for everyone`

These structures often sound finished without explaining fit, style, or trade-offs.

## Excessive rhetorical contrast

Review repeated forms such as:

- `not X, but Y`
- `less like X, more like Y`
- `not only X; also Y`
- `the route does not care...`

One contrast can sharpen a point. Repeated contrasts make the catalog feel templated.

## Metaphor stacking

Flag copy that gives a route teeth, a voice, an attitude, a courtroom, and a personality test in the same card. Keep at most one useful image, then state the route type, fit, or consideration directly.

## Poetic fragments

Review fragments that create mood without meaning, especially in UI or route facts: `Big scale. Thin air. Pure commitment.` Fragments are acceptable only when the surrounding copy supplies concrete information.

## Unsupported authority

Flag words such as `classic`, `world-class`, `friendly`, `safe`, `reliable`, `high quality`, `popular`, `best`, or `perfect` when the claim has no named source or clear editorial framing.

## Internal terminology

Never expose these labels on public pages:

- `pick candidate`
- `needs upgrade`
- `imported index`
- `no outbound link`
- `editorial draft`

Also review `verification status`, `source-backed`, and raw provider fields. Translate them into user value or place them in an appropriate source disclosure.

## Ambiguous numbers and labels

Flag percentages that do not say what is being measured. `94% match` is weaker than `94% preference match`, followed by a short explanation of the dimensions and limitations.

## Repeated catalog templates

Flag clusters of:

- `Choose it when...`
- `Pick it when...`
- `This card is for climbers who...`
- `It suits days when...`

The underlying decision information may be useful. Vary the sentence structure and remove references to the UI card itself.

## Translation artifacts

Flag:

- literal English syntax in Chinese;
- unnecessary English nouns such as `card`, `session`, or `ego` inside otherwise Chinese copy;
- different certainty levels between languages;
- a metaphor that works in one language but becomes awkward in the other;
- mojibake, replacement characters, or malformed punctuation.

## Review question

For every flagged pattern, ask: “What concrete information would the reader lose if this phrase were removed?” If the answer is “none,” revise it.
