# ClimbAtlas Editorial Examples

These examples are based on copy currently present in the repository. They demonstrate the editorial standard only; they are not approved production edits. Any factual statement still requires the same source check as live content.

## Homepage: keep the promise, reduce abstraction

**Before**

> Discover routes, crags, and climbing experiences shaped around your movement, motivation, and appetite for adventure.

**After**

> Compare destinations by climbing style, trip preferences, and the kind of day you want on the rock.

**Why:** The revision explains the product action and avoids promising that experiences are “shaped around” the user.

**Chinese**

> 按攀登风格、旅行偏好和你想要的岩壁体验，比较不同目的地。

## Destination: one image, then decision value

**Before**

> Yosemite is the big-wall daydream with granite teeth. Come here when you want history, patience, and a route choice that feels bigger than the number.

**After**

> Yosemite is defined by granite climbing across boulders, cracks, and long wall routes. It best suits climbers who value history, technical systems, and objectives that reward patient planning.

**Why:** The revision retains character but makes style and fit easier to scan. Verify the scope of climbing and historical framing before publication.

**Chinese**

> 优胜美地以花岗岩攀登著称，涵盖抱石、裂缝和长距离岩壁路线。这里更适合重视攀岩历史、技术系统和耐心规划的攀岩者。

## Route card: remove stacked metaphor

**Before**

> Omaha Beach is useful when you want the Red River Gorge feeling without immediately volunteering your forearms for a trial by fire.

**After**

> An approachable Red River Gorge sport route for climbers building endurance and movement rhythm before choosing harder objectives.

**Why:** The revision answers route type, fit, and use without turning forearm fatigue into the main idea. `Approachable` and the training implication require verification or clear editorial qualification.

**Chinese**

> 一条相对亲近的红河峡谷运动攀路线，适合在挑战更难目标前积累耐力和动作节奏。

## Route decision hint: talk to the climber, not the card

**Before**

> This card is for climbers who want to practice making peace with the pump before it starts writing poetry.

**After**

> Consider it when the day’s goal is sustained climbing and deliberate pacing rather than a short power test.

**Why:** The revision keeps the decision useful and avoids UI self-reference. The route’s endurance character must be sourced or clearly marked as editorial judgment.

**Chinese**

> 如果今天想练持续攀爬和主动控节奏，而不是短时间爆发，可以考虑这条线路。

## UI: hide internal workflow language

**Before**

> source-backed route index

**After**

> Route overview

**Why:** Most users need to know what the page contains, not how the import pipeline classifies it. Source details remain available in the source section.

**Chinese**

> 路线概览

## Climbing DNA archetype: expressive but conditional

**Before**

> You climb to make the world feel larger.

**After**

> You are often drawn to climbing that brings new places and unfamiliar rock into the experience.

**Why:** The revision keeps identity value while describing a tendency rather than declaring a fixed motive.

**Chinese**

> 你常常会被陌生岩石和新目的地吸引，也希望攀岩能带你看见更大的世界。

## DNA Match: define the percentage

**Before**

> 94% match

**After**

> 94% preference match · strongest alignment: exploration and social energy

> Based on your quiz preferences and the destination profile. This score does not assess ability, conditions, or safety.

**Chinese**

> 94% 偏好匹配 · 最契合：探索倾向与社交氛围

> 结果来自你的测试偏好和目的地资料，不代表能力、实时条件或安全判断。

## Chinese localization: preserve intent, not English syntax

**English source**

> Enough challenge to feel alive, enough ease to stay curious.

**Natural Chinese**

> 有挑战，但仍留得下从容和好奇心。

**Why:** The Chinese keeps the balance and tone without mirroring the repeated English construction.
