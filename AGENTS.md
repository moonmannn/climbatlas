# ClimbAtlas Editorial Safety

Apply these rules whenever a task creates or changes public ClimbAtlas content.

- Do not invent route facts, history, movement, rock quality, protection quality, safety information, access information, conditions, popularity, beta, or user opinion.
- Preserve unknown information as unavailable. Never make a guess read like a verified fact.
- Keep sourced facts, ClimbAtlas editorial judgment, and inferred Climbing DNA attributes visibly distinct.
- Do not expose internal workflow labels on public pages, including `pick candidate`, `needs upgrade`, `imported index`, `no outbound link`, and `editorial draft`.
- Keep English and Chinese meaning aligned. Write natural Chinese instead of translating English syntax literally.
- Do not copy route descriptions, beta, topos, approach or descent notes, protection details, comments, ratings, guidebook text, or unlicensed images from external sources.
- Treat match percentages as preference alignment, not ability, safety, readiness, or a promise of enjoyment.
- When factual support is incomplete, retain the limitation in the data or UI instead of improving the claim through copy.

Use the repository skills `climbatlas-editorial-copy` for drafting and `climbatlas-copy-review` for audits. Detailed guidance lives in `docs/content/`.
